Civil Legal Advice Privacy Statement

In order to provide a service to you, Civil Legal Advice (CLA) may need to view, collect, record and hold (‘process’) personal data about you.

Personal data is information about you that can be used to identify you in some way e.g. your name or telephone number. You can look at the full legal definition.

CLA is committed to protecting your personal data and respecting your privacy. We are legally bound by the Data Protection Act 1998 to ensure that we only use your personal data for the reasons we tell you about. We must also hold it securely.

This statement explains how CLA will process any personal data that we collect from you or that you provide to us.

What is Civil Legal Advice?

CLA is a national adviceline for England and Wales, paid for by Legal Aid.

The Legal Aid Agency (LAA) is responsible for the CLA service and the Ministry of Justice (MoJ) is the Data Controller for the purposes of the Data Protection Act 1998. They are responsible for determining the purposes for which and the manner in which any personal data are, or are to be processed.

For a more comprehensive explanation see this guide.

The LAA works with other organisations who deliver the CLA service. For example when you first make contact with CLA you will communicate with the CLA Operator Service, which is delivered by Agilisys (a contractor employed by the LAA to [ ]).

If after contacting the CLA Operator Service it is determined that you qualify for the CLA specialist advice service you may be put through to one of a number of CLA Specialist Advice Providers. If you do not qualify to receive CLA specialist advice we will inform you accordingly and direct you to alternative help.

These organisations are Data Processors for the purposes of the Data Protection Act 1998 which means that they process data on behalf of the LAA and MoJ.

The CLA service is committed to following this Privacy Statement. CLA Specialists must also adhere to their own internal privacy policy.

What data will CLA collect?

CLA will only collect the minimum amount of information necessary in order to deliver the CLA service to you.

The kind of information CLA may collect about you includes:

  • Personal details including your name, address, date of birth, contact details, diversity monitoring information and national insurance number;
  • Financial information needed to determine if you qualify for legal aid;
  • Reason for contacting CLA, such as the nature of your problem or specific details about your situation;
  • Details of any specific needs that you may have in order to access the CLA service;
  • Responses to any customer satisfaction survey or details of any feedback, complaints or requests for a review of our decision about whether you qualify for legal aid.

The amount of information CLA will collect will depend on whether you qualify for the CLA service and/or your specific needs. For example you will be asked to provide more information before we can transfer you through to a CLA Specialist.

What does CLA use the data for?

CLA collects and records information about you to:

  • Work out whether you qualify for legal aid
  • Provide information and/or advice services to you
  • Save you from repeating information every time you contact us
  • Help us monitor and improve our service
  • Build statistics about the CLA service, monitor fraud and justify public expenditure. This information is collated in an anonymous format.
  • Collect views on the CLA service and any improvements considered necessary. Where consent is provided CLA User contact details may be passed onto an independent research agency.
  • Prevent or detect crime or fraud

Given the purposes for which your personal data may be used (as referred to above) it is essential that the information you provide is accurate and complete.

How does CLA collect your personal data and keep it safe?

CLA may collect personal data about you by phone, online, email or post (see table below). Any personal data will be recorded and stored within a secure system that meets required Government standards.

CLA will be able to view and verify the information that you provide and where necessary will amend it to maintain its accuracy.

The minimum amount of personal data that CLA will ask to collect is your name and one other piece of personal information that could be used to identify you e.g. postcode, date of birth or telephone number.

All personal data will be stored on secure servers in the UK. It may also be stored outside of Europe, where it could be viewed by our staff or specialist providers.

By submitting your personal data, you agree to information being recorded, used and stored as outlined in this Privacy Statement.

How we collect and store your personal data

Phone When you call CLA we will record relevant personal data that you provide within a secure digital system.

We may also record your calls. But you can choose to opt out of this or choose whether or not to continue with the call.

If you leave CLA a voice message we will record the minimum amount of information required in order to contact you back.
Online When you contact CLA online the personal data that you provide will be recorded within a secure digital system.

Please note that transmitting information over the internet is generally not completely secure, and CLA can’t guarantee the security of your data. Any data you transmit is at your own risk. CLA have procedures and security features in place to keep your data secure once we receive it.
Online Transmitting information via email is generally not completely secure, and CLA can’t guarantee the security of your data. Any data you transmit is at your own risk.

CLA have procedures and security features in place to keep your data secure once we receive it.

When you email CLA, relevant personal data that you provide will be recorded within a secure digital system.

CLA may monitor and store both your incoming and outgoing emails. We may also use blocking software,
Post When CLA receives post we may record relevant personal data included within a secure digital system.

CLA may also retain original hard-copies of information submitted by you. This must be stored in a locked filing cabinet. Digital copies of letters may be scanned and stored.

Keeping your personal data safe from others

Repeat Contacts CLA will not disclose any personal information to anyone contacting the CLA service until they have successfully verified that their identity matches that of an existing record.
Third-parties CLA will accept contact made by a third-party on behalf of another person.

CLA must record the relationship between the third-party and the individual seeking advice and whether they are authorised to act on their behalf.

They must also agree a password to verify the identity of the third-party if they contact the service again.
Outgoing Communications CLA may need to contact you directly.

In order to protect privacy CLA will always check with you what forms of outgoing communication are acceptable and appropriate and whether messages can be left.

When making outgoing calls to you CLA will ensure that:
  • Call backs are only made where you have agreed to this. Authorised third parties acting on your behalf can give agreement.
  • Where a call back is made to you and there is no answer and a message taker is available, no message will be left unless you have previously given specific instructions that this would be acceptable.
  • Where the phone is answered and someone other than you answers the phone and says that you are unavailable or asks who is calling, CLA will state that the call is confidential and will not say that they are calling from CLA.

How we use cookies

When you use our online services we may store a small file, known as a cookie, on your PC or digital device.

You may see a message before we store a cookie on your computer.

The cookies are not used to identify you personally.

We use these cookies to:

  • Help your PC or digital device to interact with us more quickly
  • Measure how you use our service so it can be updated and improved based on your needs
  • Remember the notifications you've seen so that we don't show them to you again
  • Remember how you progress through our service. These cookies don't store your personal data and are deleted once you've completed the service.
  • Protect your security by ensuring that third parties cannot create sites that pose as a gov.uk service in an attempt to gather your information.

You can read more about how cookies are used on gov.uk.

You can read about how to manage cookies.

When will we share information about you outside CLA?

Confidentiality means ensuring information is only accessible by those allowed to have access to it.

We want to keep your contact with CLA confidential, which means you can feel safe talking to us. But we sometimes need to share some information outside CLA which we explain below.

In order to provide you with the best service that we can we may share some information about you with the organisations mentioned below. But we will only do this if you have given us consent when the operator asks for it.

Who Why
External research organisations engaged by LAA or MoJ To inform future government policy on legal aid. Such research will not identify individuals whose data has been used for research purposes.
Other government organisations To verify information provided by you and check whether you qualify for legal aid.
Other advice organisations To help you get further help and advice from appropriate organisations.

In exceptional circumstances we may share limited information without your consent, for example if it is a legal requirement or a court compels us to do so.

If we do this we will wherever possible:

  • Talk to you first
  • Give you as much control as possible over how we do this
  • Only share information on a ‘need to know’ basis.
Who Why
Other government organisations To prevent fraud and help detect crime.

Or where it is required by law or by a court order
External protection organisations If we are very worried about your safety or the safety of other people we may need to let others know.

For example if we think it necessary in order to protect a child, young person or vulnerable adult from abuse or if we think that there is an immediate risk of harm.

Requests to view and amend CLA Data

You have the right to request details about the personal information CLA holds about you.

You can also request us to correct any personal information if it is found to be inaccurate or out of date.

Requests to view personal data held by CLA

If you want to view your record, please make a request in writing including your.

  • Full name
  • Address
  • Date of birth
  • What you contacted CLA about
  • When you contacted CLA
  • What information you want to see
  • Any additional information that might help us to check your identity e.g. CLA case reference number. This is to help us protect your privacy by ensuring that we only give data out to the person it relates to (or their appropriately authorised representative).

Please note that we cannot give out any information unless the details that you provide match our records. If you used a fake name or pseudonym when you contacted CLA you will need to prove your identity.

If you gave us enough information to identify you, CLA will send you your personal information as soon as possible but within a maximum of 40 days.

You can contact us by email at data.access@justice.gsi.gov.uk or in writing to

Data Access and Compliance Unit Postal Point 10.31 Floor 10 102 Petty France London SW1H 9AJ

Requests to delete personal data

CLA will only keep a record of your personal data where you have consented to this and we will handle it carefully.

We will consider requests to delete some or all of your personal data but please note that as a minimum CLA will usually retain:

  • For everyone that uses our service we will retain a name and one other piece of personal data e.g. date of birth and details of what we did for you
  • For everyone that asks CLA to check whether they qualify for legal aid in order to seek advice from a CLA Specialist we will also retain details of any financial information and case details used to prove that you qualify for legal aid.

Please note that if you qualify to receive advice from a CLA Specialist, they will also need to retain additional personal data about you within their own Case Handling System. Such data shall be sufficient to justify the provision of legal aid advice and meet professional requirements.

Changes to the statement

This policy is under regular review. Any changes made to the privacy policy will be posted here and, where appropriate, CLA staff will be notified by email.

Complaints about this statement

If you would like to make a complaint about this statement and how it applies to you then please contact CLA on 0345 345 4 345.
