Find out if Civil Legal Advice can help you

If you qualify for legal aid, you may be able to get help from Civil Legal Advice (CLA), a national advice line for England and Wales.

This service will ask you general questions about your problem and detailed questions about your finances. You’ll only get guidance on whether you might qualify for legal aid - you won’t get a final decision.

If it looks like you might qualify for legal aid, you can speak to a Civil Legal Advice operator about your problem. If CLA can’t help, you’ll get suggestions of other places you could go for advice.

Before you start

To use this service, you’ll need details of the following, for you and your partner, if you have one:

  • your income, tax and National Insurance contributions
  • mortgage statements and current valuation for any property you own
  • any savings, investments or valuable items
  • any benefits you receive

Legal aid is different in Scotland and Northern Ireland.

For more detail on whether your specific problem is covered by legal aid, use the GOV.UK legal aid checker

If you’re under 18, please contact Civil Legal Advice.

If you’ve been charged with a crime, this service doesn’t apply to you. Ask your solicitor about criminal legal aid.
