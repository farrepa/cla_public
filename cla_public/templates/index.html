Legal aid can help pay for legal advice, family mediation or representation in a court or tribunal. It only covers some types of problem.

{% endblock %} {% block inner_content %}

To get legal aid, you must qualify financially and have a relevant problem.

If you qualify, you may be able to speak to a Civil Legal Advice operator about your problem at the end of this service. Civil Legal Advice is a national advice line for England and Wales, funded by legal aid.

Start

Before you start

To use this service, you’ll need:

  • last month’s payslips (if you’re working)
  • last month’s bank statements
  • details of any benefits you got last month

You’ll only get guidance on whether or not you can get legal aid - you won’t get a final decision.

Legal aid is different in Scotland and Northern Ireland.

For more detail on whether your specific problem is covered by legal aid, use the GOV.UK legal aid checker

This service is only for adults. If you’re under 18, talk to a solicitor about legal aid.

If you’ve been charged with a crime, this service doesn’t apply to you. Ask your solicitor about criminal legal aid.
